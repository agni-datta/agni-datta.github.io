TCS Resources

When I was starting out, these were the resources I found most helpful. After talking with other beginners in the theory community, this list has become the standard go-to.

Textbooks

  • Arora and Barak — Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach The definitive graduate-level text on complexity theory.
  • Sipser — Introduction to the Theory of Computation Accessible undergraduate introduction to automata, computability, and complexity.
  • Motwani and Raghavan — Randomized Algorithms The classic text on algorithms that use randomness.
  • Cormen et al. — Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) Essential reference for algorithms and data structures.
  • Katz and Lindell — Introduction to Modern Cryptography A rigorous and widely used introduction to modern cryptography.
  • Goldreich — Foundations of Cryptography (Vol. 1 & 2) Extensive treatment of the theoretical underpinnings of cryptography. Does not cover the most recent developments.
  • Goldreich — Computational Complexity: A Conceptual Perspective A conceptual approach to complexity theory.
  • Aaronson — Quantum Computing Since Democritus Quantum computation and TCS more broadly. More engaging than Nielsen & Chuang, though the latter remains the standard reference.
  • Graham, Knuth, and Patashnik — Concrete Mathematics Essential mathematical foundations for computer science.
  • Wigderson — Mathematics and Computation A comprehensive account of the connections between mathematics and theoretical computer science.
  • Nielsen and Chuang — Quantum Computation and Quantum Information The standard textbook for quantum computation and information theory.
  • Alon and Spencer — The Probabilistic Method The definitive reference on the probabilistic method in combinatorics and TCS.

Courses & lecture notes

  • O'Donnell — CS Theory Toolkit (CMU) Essential mathematical tools for TCS.
  • Goldreich — Lecture notes In-depth notes on complexity, cryptography, and foundations.
  • Watrous — Theory of Quantum Information Widely recommended for quantum computation.
  • Zhandry — Recent Developments in Program Obfuscation (Princeton) Techniques and results in program obfuscation.
  • Zhandry — Quantum Cryptography (Princeton) Cryptography based on quantum mechanics.

Research & conferences

  • ECCC Online archive of research reports in computational complexity.
  • arXiv — cs.CC, cs.DS, cs.LO, cs.CR Primary preprint server for complexity, logic, and cryptography.
  • IACR ePrint Definitive archive of cryptography preprints.
  • STOC One of the two premier annual conferences in theoretical computer science.
  • FOCS The other premier annual conference in TCS.
  • IACR Conferences — CRYPTO, EUROCRYPT, TCC Main annual conferences of the International Association for Cryptologic Research.

Blogs

  • Shtetl-Optimized Scott Aaronson on quantum computing and complexity theory.
  • Gödel's Lost Letter and P=NP Ken Regan and Dick Lipton on developments in complexity theory.
  • Windows On Theory Boaz Barak on complexity, cryptography, and foundations of machine learning.
  • Computational Complexity Lance Fortnow and Bill Gasarch on complexity theory and related topics.
  • Goldreich — My Choices Personal selections of interesting work in theoretical computer science.

Reference & Q&A

  • TCS Stack Exchange Q&A for students and researchers in theoretical computer science.
  • Cryptography Stack Exchange Q&A for cryptography and cryptanalysis.
  • The Complexity Zoo Wiki cataloguing hundreds of computational complexity classes.

Communities & aggregators

  • Theory of Computing Blog Aggregator A single feed of posts from prominent TCS blogs.
  • SIGACT ACM Special Interest Group on Algorithms and Computation Theory.
  • IACR International Association for Cryptologic Research — news, publications, and conferences.
